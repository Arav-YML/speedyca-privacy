Privacy Policy

Application: Speedy CA (package com.epicmindlabs.speedycurrentaffairs)
Publisher: Epic Mind Labs

This Privacy Policy describes how Epic Mind Labs ("we", "us", "our") handles information in connection with the Speedy CA mobile application (the "App"), available on Google Play. The App is distributed free of charge and is supported by in‑app advertising. By installing or using the App you accept the terms of this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with this policy, please uninstall the App and stop using it.

1. The Short Version

  • We do not ask you to create an account, sign in, or hand over any name, email, phone number, or address.
  • All your study state — last‑read page, mock‑test attempts, history, theme and language preference — stays locally on your device.
  • The only network calls the App itself makes are: (a) downloading the bundled current‑affairs PDF library once from Our public GitHub release, (b) checking for app updates via Google Play, and (c) requesting ads from Google AdMob.
  • Ads are served by Google AdMob, which independently collects standard advertising identifiers as described in its own policy.
  • We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13.

2. Information the App Itself Collects

The App does not run any backend server we operate and does not transmit personally identifying information to Epic Mind Labs. Everything listed below is stored in the App's private storage on your own device and is removed when you uninstall the App or clear its data from Android Settings.

  • Reading progress — the last page you viewed for each PDF topic.
  • Mock‑test attempts & history — your answers, score, time taken and timestamps.
  • App preferences — selected app language (Hindi / English), theme (system / light / dark), and a flag indicating whether the welcome screen has been shown.
  • Cached content — the current‑affairs PDFs themselves, extracted once into the App's internal folder so they can be read offline thereafter.

None of the items above leave your device. We have no way to read, copy, back‑up, or recover them.

3. Permissions We Request and Why

  • INTERNET — to download the one‑time PDF library, fetch ads, and check for app updates / in‑app review prompts.
  • ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE — to detect whether the device is online and show a friendly "no internet" screen when it is not.

The App requests no other dangerous or sensitive permissions. It does not access your contacts, calendar, camera, microphone, location, photos, SMS, call logs, accounts, or files outside its own private folder.

4. Third‑Party Services Used Inside the App

To keep the App free we rely on a small number of well‑known SDKs. Each of these services operates under its own privacy policy:

  • Google AdMob — serves banner, interstitial, rewarded and native ads, and may collect advertising identifiers, coarse device data and ad‑interaction signals to deliver and measure ads. Google Ads policy.
  • Google Play Services & Play Core — used for in‑app updates and in‑app review prompts. Google privacy policy.
  • GitHub (static asset hosting) — the App downloads its initial PDF library from a public GitHub release URL. GitHub may log the IP address that issues that one HTTP request, as is standard for any web request. GitHub privacy statement.

We do not control how these third parties handle data they collect under their own policies. We do not receive a personally identifying copy of any of it.

5. Advertising & Advertising IDs

The App is monetised through advertising delivered by Google AdMob. AdMob may use your device's advertising identifier (AAID) and standard request metadata (device model, OS version, language, coarse region inferred from IP, ad interaction events) to serve and measure ads. You can reset your advertising ID, or opt out of personalised ads entirely, from your Android device settings (Settings → Google → Ads). Doing so does not remove ads from the App but makes them non‑personalised.

6. Crash & Diagnostic Data

The App does not embed any third‑party crash‑reporting or analytics SDK (Firebase Analytics, Crashlytics, Sentry, Mixpanel, etc.). If the App crashes, Google Play may collect a standard ANR / crash trace at the operating‑system level if you have opted in to share usage data with Google. That collection is governed by Google's privacy policy, not ours.

7. How We Store and Protect Your Data

Every piece of data the App writes is stored inside its private application sandbox on your device. We do not operate a backend that holds your data and we do not transmit it to ourselves. While we follow reasonable engineering practices to keep the App safe (release signing, tamper checks on critical components, and HTTPS‑only network calls), no software is 100% secure and we cannot guarantee absolute security for data that already lives on your device.

8. Children's Privacy

The App is intended for general audiences — primarily students preparing for Indian civil‑service and state‑level examinations — and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us at the address below and we will take appropriate action.

9. Data Retention & Deletion

Because all App data lives only on your device, you control retention. You can remove it at any time by:

  • Going to Android Settings → Apps → Speedy CA → Storage → Clear data, or
  • Uninstalling the App.

For data held by the third‑party services listed in Section 4 (e.g. ad identifiers), follow the deletion / reset options in each provider's privacy policy.

10. Your Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights under applicable data‑protection laws (such as the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 in India, GDPR in the EEA/UK, or CCPA in California) to request access, correction, restriction, portability, or deletion of personal data we hold about you. Because we do not hold a personally identifying copy of your App data on our servers, most such requests will be satisfied by the on‑device controls described above; for anything else, contact us.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy as the App evolves. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will always reflect the most recent revision. Material changes will also be highlighted in the App's next update notes on Google Play. Continued use of the App after a change takes effect constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
